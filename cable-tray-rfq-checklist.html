What to include in a cable tray RFQ for a faster quotation

A complete RFQ helps the supplier quote the correct cable tray, accessories, packing and delivery terms. This checklist is useful for distributors, contractors and overseas buyers.

Updated: June 14, 2026Reading time: 4 minutesTopic: Cable tray procurement

Many cable tray quotations are delayed because the first inquiry does not include enough technical information. A clear RFQ reduces repeated questions and helps the supplier prepare a more accurate price, packing plan and lead time.

1. Product type and application

State the cable tray type first: perforated cable tray, ladder cable tray, wire mesh cable tray, solid bottom tray, cable trunking or accessories. If you are not sure, describe the cable route, load and environment.

2. Size, thickness and length

Include width, height, thickness and standard length. For example, a buyer may request 200 mm width, 100 mm height, 1.5 mm thickness and 3 m length. If several sizes are needed, a size schedule is better than a long message.

3. Material and surface finish

Common requirements include pre-galvanized steel, hot-dip galvanized steel, stainless steel 304, stainless steel 316, aluminum or powder coated steel. If the project specification has a zinc thickness or color requirement, include it in the RFQ.

4. Accessories and route fittings

Most projects need more than straight tray sections. Add bends, tees, crosses, reducers, covers, clamps, brackets, splice plates and fasteners if required. Drawings are the best way to avoid missing fittings.

5. Quantity and packing requirement

Provide quantity by size and by accessory type. For export orders, also mention whether you need bundle packing, pallet packing, wooden crate packing, labels, shipping marks or mixed-size loading.

6. Destination and trade terms

State destination port or delivery address and the preferred trade term such as EXW, FOB, CIF or DDP if known. This helps the supplier estimate carton, pallet and container space.

Simple RFQ template

Product: perforated cable tray
Size: width x height x thickness x length
Material/finish: hot-dip galvanized steel
Quantity: straight trays plus accessories
Documents: invoice, packing list, test report if needed
Destination: port or address
Attachments: drawings, BOQ or project specification

When these details are ready, the supplier can respond with a clearer quotation, estimated weight, packing method and production lead time.
