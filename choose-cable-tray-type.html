Cable tray selection guide

How to choose the right cable tray type for an electrical project

The right cable tray depends on cable weight, ventilation, site environment, protection level and installation method. This guide helps buyers compare common tray types before preparing an RFQ.

Updated: June 14, 2026Reading time: 5 minutesTopic: Cable tray selection

Cable tray is not a single product. Perforated cable tray, ladder cable tray, wire mesh cable tray and solid bottom tray are designed for different cable routes and project conditions. Choosing the correct type early can reduce installation changes, avoid over-specification and make the quotation clearer.

Perforated cable tray: balanced support and ventilation

Perforated cable tray is widely used in factories, commercial buildings, control rooms and general power distribution routes. The side and bottom holes provide ventilation, drainage and convenient cable fixing points while still giving cables continuous support.

Choose perforated tray when the project needs a practical balance of protection, airflow and easy inspection. It is often selected for medium-duty power cables, control cables and mixed building service routes.

Ladder cable tray: heavy load and long span routes

Ladder cable tray is suitable when cable weight is higher or support spans are longer. The side rails and rungs make the tray strong while keeping the cable route open for heat dissipation and easy cable tying.

Choose ladder tray for power plants, industrial corridors, substations, mechanical rooms and long straight runs where strength and cable ventilation are important.

Wire mesh cable tray: fast installation and flexible routing

Wire mesh cable tray is commonly used for data cable, low voltage cable and light-duty routes. It can be cut and shaped on site, which helps installers handle route changes without waiting for many custom fittings.

Choose wire mesh tray for data rooms, communication cabling, retail projects, ceiling voids and routes that need fast installation with good airflow.

Solid bottom tray: extra protection and clean containment

Solid bottom cable tray gives cables more physical protection from dust, falling objects and visual exposure. A cover can be added when the project needs a cleaner appearance or stronger containment.

Choose solid bottom tray for areas where cable exposure should be limited, where dust protection matters, or where the project specification asks for covered cable containment.

Quick selection checklist

  • Use perforated tray for common industrial and commercial cable support.
  • Use ladder tray for heavy power cables, ventilation and long spans.
  • Use wire mesh tray for data cables and fast on-site adjustment.
  • Use solid bottom tray with cover when extra protection or clean appearance is required.
  • Confirm width, height, thickness, material, finish and accessories before asking for price.
Buyer tip: For export quotations, send drawings or a schedule with tray type, size, finish, quantity, bends, tees, reducers, covers and destination port. This helps the supplier calculate production, packing and freight volume more accurately.
